1. What Fairgo is

Fairgo is a technology intermediary that connects independent drivers with riders. Fairgo is not a transport carrier and does not provide transportation. Where drivers set their own fares, the transport contract is between the rider and the driver; Fairgo provides the matching technology and charges drivers a technology service fee (1%).

2. Payments

Riders pay for rides using the payment methods offered in their market (e.g. Apple Pay, Google Pay, card or cash). Fairgo does not take custody of ride fares. Fairgo invoices drivers a monthly technology service fee. Applicable taxes are added where required by law.

3. Driver independence

Drivers are independent. They may decline requests without penalty, are not required to work exclusively with Fairgo, and account actions are rules-based and appealable.

4. Cancellations

Either party may cancel a trip. A rider who cancels after a short grace window, once a driver has committed to the trip, may be charged a small cancellation fee shown in the app. Drivers are never penalised for cancelling.
