Privacy Policy

Last updated: . Draft — pending legal review.

This policy explains what data Fairgo processes and why, across the Fairgo (rider) and Fairgo Driver apps. We aim to collect the minimum needed to run a safe, working ride-hailing service, and we self-host our backend for data sovereignty.

Data we process

  • Account: email (and phone if provided) to sign you in and contact you.
  • Location: precise GPS while you use the app — riders for pickup and live trip tracking; drivers for availability and matching. Background location is used only while you are online as a driver.
  • Ride data: pickup/dropoff, route, price, timestamps, ratings.
  • Driver compliance: identity and licensing documents where required by local law, used solely for verification.
  • Payments: handled by the payment method you choose; Fairgo does not take custody of ride fares and does not store full card numbers.
  • Notifications: a device token to send ride updates (offers, arrival, cancellations). You can disable notifications any time in iOS Settings.

How we use it

To match riders and drivers, calculate fares, provide live tracking and safety features, meet legal and tax obligations, prevent fraud, and improve reliability. We do not sell your personal data.

Sharing

Between the matched rider and driver (limited profile, live location during a trip), with authorities where legally required, and with processors that operate the service under contract (e.g. the payment processor). Some markets legally require sharing certain data with local transport authorities; we disclose this in-app where it applies.

Your rights

Subject to your region (e.g. GDPR / nLPD), you may access, correct, export or delete your data, and object to certain processing. Contact us via the Support page.

Retention & security

We keep data only as long as needed for the service and legal obligations, then delete or anonymize it. Access is restricted and data is protected in transit and at rest.

This document is a draft placeholder and does not constitute legal advice. The final policy will reflect the exact data practices per market and be reviewed by counsel.
