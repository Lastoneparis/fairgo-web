Privacy Policy

Last updated: 20 July 2026. Applies to the Fairgo (rider) and Fairgo Driver apps.

This policy explains exactly what personal data Fairgo processes, why, who else sees it, and what control you have. We have written it to describe what the apps actually do, including the parts that are inconvenient to admit. We collect the minimum needed to run a safe ride-hailing service, we run our own backend rather than renting a cloud platform, and we use no advertising, analytics, attribution or tracking SDKs of any kind.

1. Who is responsible

Fairgo is operated by Hugo Moriceau (sole trader), Switzerland, who is the data controller for the processing described here.

Contact for any privacy question or request: hugomoriceau@icloud.com, or via the Support page. A postal address is available on request to that address.

2. What we collect

Everyone

  • Account — email address, and your name and phone number if you provide them. If you use Sign in with Apple, we receive the email address Apple gives us (which may be a private relay address) and the name you choose to share.
  • Precise location — GPS coordinates from your device. For riders, while you are using the app to set a pickup point and follow a trip. For drivers, continuously while you are online, including in the background, because matching requires knowing where available drivers are.
  • Trip records — pickup and drop-off coordinates and addresses, the route taken, times, distance, price, tip, ratings and any cancellation.
  • A second-by-second GPS trail of each trip, recorded while the trip is in progress, so a trip can be reconstructed for safety, disputes and fare accuracy.
  • Messages you send in the in-app chat, and reports or complaints you submit.
  • Device notification token, so we can send you ride updates.
  • Crash diagnostics — error messages and technical stack traces if the app fails. These are stored on our own servers, not sent to any third-party crash service.

Riders

  • Saved places — any Home, Work or other address you save, with its coordinates.
  • Safety contact — if you add one, the name and phone number of another person. Please only add someone who is happy for you to share their details. We never disclose your safety contact to your driver.
  • Accessibility — if you request a wheelchair-accessible vehicle, we record that requirement on that trip in order to match you with a suitable driver. This may indicate information about your health, and we process it only to fulfil your request.
  • Profile photo and rating, if you add a photo.

Drivers

  • Identity and licensing documents — driving licence, identity document, insurance and any other document your local rules require, plus the details read from them (name, document number, expiry date).
  • Vehicle details — make, model, colour and registration plate.
  • Payout and tax information — your Stripe account identifier, whether your identity checks are complete, and tax data where the law requires us to collect it.
  • Your last known location while online, refreshed regularly even when you are stationary. Be aware this is often your home address; it is erased when you delete your account.
  • Your published tariff, availability and rating.

3. Why we process it, and on what legal basis

  • To provide the service — matching, pricing, navigation, live tracking, chat, receipts and support. Basis: performance of our contract with you.
  • Safety and security — trip traces, PIN verification, trip sharing, fraud and abuse prevention, rate limiting. Basis: our legitimate interest in a safe service, and the vital interests of users in an emergency.
  • Driver eligibility — verifying documents and licences before a driver may carry passengers. Basis: legal obligation and our legitimate interest in passenger safety.
  • Payments, invoicing, tax and accounting — including our 1% technology fee. Basis: contract and legal obligation.
  • Accessibility matching. Basis: your explicit request.
  • Marketing messages, only if you opt in. Basis: consent, withdrawable at any time.

We do not sell personal data, we do not share it with data brokers, and we do not profile you for advertising.

4. Who else sees your data

The other person on your trip

Riders see the driver's name, photo, vehicle, registration plate and rating. Drivers see the rider's first name, photo and rating, and the pickup and drop-off points. While a trip is live, each party can call the other on their real phone number — we do not currently mask numbers. Driver and rider profile photos are served from public storage, which means the photo file itself can be opened by anyone who has its exact link.

If you share your trip

The rider app can generate a trip-sharing link. Anyone who holds that link can open it without logging in and will see the pickup and drop-off addresses and coordinates, the vehicle and its full registration plate, the driver's first name and the driver's live position. The link stops returning any of this as soon as the trip ends. Only share it with people you trust.

Service providers

  • Stripe (payments). Fairgo never takes custody of your fare and never sees or stores your full card number. The driver is the merchant and is paid directly; we collect only our 1% fee. Drivers enter their identity and bank details directly into Stripe's own onboarding pages, not into Fairgo.
  • Mindee (document checking, France). When a driver uploads an identity or licensing document, the document image itself is sent to Mindee to read the name, number and expiry automatically. If automatic checking is unavailable, the document is instead reviewed by a human reviewer at Fairgo.
  • Apple (push notifications and Sign in with Apple). To deliver a notification, your device token and the notification text are sent to Apple. For chat notifications this includes the sender's first name and the first part of the message.
  • OpenStreetMap Foundation and the OSRM routing service (address search and routing). When you type a destination, the text you type is sent to the address-search service, and your pickup and drop-off coordinates are sent to the routing service, directly from your device — which also reveals your device's IP address to them.
  • CARTO (map images). Displaying the map requests map tiles for the area you are looking at, which reveals your approximate position and IP address to that provider.

Authorities

We disclose data where we are legally required to, or where it is necessary to protect someone's life or safety. Some markets require ride-hailing operators to give local transport authorities access to operating data as a condition of the licence; where that applies to your market, we will say so in the app before you use the service there.

5. Where your data is stored, and transfers

Our database and files are held on servers we operate at a hosting provider in Munich, Germany (Contabo GmbH), inside the European Union. We do not use a third-party cloud platform for your ride data.

Some of the providers in section 4 are located outside your country — in particular Stripe and Apple (Ireland and the United States), Mindee (France), and the map and routing services. Where a transfer leaves the EU or Switzerland, it relies on the European Commission's standard contractual clauses or an adequacy decision.

6. How long we keep it, and what deleting your account really does

You can delete your account at any time from Account → Delete account in either app. We want to be precise about what that does, because "delete" is often overstated.

Erased: your profile name, phone number, profile photo, safety contact, saved addresses, driver identity documents and the details read from them, your last known location, vehicle details, payout and tax identifiers, notification tokens, your credits, the reports you filed, your crash diagnostics, and the chat messages you sent. Your login is permanently disabled and your email address is removed.

Retained: the trip records themselves — including pickup and drop-off addresses and coordinates, the recorded route trail, fares and invoices — are kept, no longer carrying your name, because we are required to keep financial and transport records. Messages the other person sent you remain visible to them in their own copy of the conversation.

We keep those records for as long as tax, accounting and transport law requires in the market concerned, and then delete them. If you want to know the exact period that applies to you, ask us.

7. Your rights

Under the EU GDPR and the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection you can ask us to give you a copy of your data, correct it, delete it, restrict or object to how we use it, and receive it in a portable form. Where we rely on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time. We answer requests within one month.

Write to hugomoriceau@icloud.com. If you are not satisfied, you can complain to your national data protection authority, or to the Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC).

8. Security

Data is encrypted in transit. Driver identity documents are held in private storage that is not publicly readable and is accessible only to the verification process. Access to the database is restricted, every table enforces row-level access rules so one user cannot read another's records, and sensitive fields such as account status and ratings can only be changed by our servers, never by an app. No system is perfectly secure, and we will notify you and the relevant authority of a breach where the law requires it.

9. Children

Fairgo is not intended for children. You must be old enough to enter into a contract in your country to use the rider app, and to hold the required licence to use the driver app.

10. Changes

If we change how we use your data we will update this page and change the date at the top, and tell you in the app where the change is significant.
